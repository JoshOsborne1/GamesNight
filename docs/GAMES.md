## Guess the Meme

**Players:** 2-20  
**Duration:** 10-15 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Easy to Hard

### Overview
Test your knowledge of internet culture! Players must identify viral memes, Vines, and internet moments from their most famous quotes.

### How to Play

1. **Host** taps NEW to display a meme quote
2. **Players** shout out their guesses
3. **Host** taps REVEAL to show the answer
4. **Host** awards points to whoever answered correctly first

### Example Questions

| Quote | Answer | Source |
|-------|--------|--------|
| "Road work ahead? Uh, yeah, I sure hope it does." | Drew Gooden | Vine |
| "It's an avocado! Thaaanks..." | Unknown | Vine |
| "Hi, welcome to Chili's" | Adam Perkins | Vine |
| "What are those?!" | Brandon Moore | Vine |
| "Ain't nobody got time for that!" | Sweet Brown | YouTube |

### Tips for Host
- Read the quote aloud with enthusiasm
- Give hints if players are stuck
- Keep the pace moving - these should be quick!

### Variations
- **Speed Round** - 5-second timer per meme
- **Act it Out** - Player must perform the meme, others guess
- **Reverse Mode** - Describe the meme without using the quote

## Geography

**Players:** 2-20  
**Duration:** 15-20 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Medium to Hard

### Overview
A comprehensive geography quiz with three sub-categories. Perfect for testing worldly knowledge!

### Sub-Modes

#### 1. Capitals
Name the capital city of a given country.

**Example:**
- **Question:** What is the capital of Australia?
- **Answer:** Canberra

#### 2. Countries
Identify countries from facts, descriptions, or outlines.

**Example:**
- **Question:** This South American country is home to Machu Picchu
- **Answer:** Peru

#### 3. Flags
Recognize countries from their flags (visual mode coming soon).

### Scoring Suggestions
- **Correct capital:** 2 points
- **Correct country:** 1 point
- **Bonus:** 3 points for answering within 5 seconds

### Tips
- Mix easy and hard questions
- Let players confer for team play
- Use for educational settings too!

## Music

**Players:** 2-20  
**Duration:** 15-20 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Easy to Hard

### Overview
Three ways to test musical knowledge - from finishing lyrics to identifying artists and performance challenges.

### Sub-Modes

#### 1. Finish the Lyric
Complete famous song lyrics.

**Example:**
- **Lyrics:** "Never gonna give you up, never gonna..."
- **Answer:** "...let you down"
- **Song:** Never Gonna Give You Up - Rick Astley

#### 2. Who Sings
Identify the artist from song titles or lyrics.

**Example:**
- **Question:** "Who sang 'Billie Jean'?"
- **Answer:** Michael Jackson

#### 3. Grab the Mic
Performance challenges!

**Example:**
- **Challenge:** "Sing the chorus of 'Sweet Caroline'"
- **Bonus:** Best performance gets extra points!

### Scoring Suggestions
- **Finish lyric:** 2 points
- **Identify artist:** 1 point
- **Performance:** 1-3 points (host decides)

### Tips for Host
- Have a backup music player for playing song snippets
- Encourage enthusiastic performances!
- Adjust difficulty based on your group's music knowledge

## Riddles

**Players:** 2-20  
**Duration:** 10-15 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Medium to Hard

### Overview
Classic and modern brain teasers that require lateral thinking.

### How to Play

1. Host reads the riddle aloud
2. Players can ask yes/no questions
3. First to solve gets the points
4. If stuck after 2 minutes, reveal the answer

### Example Riddles

**Riddle:** "I have cities, but no houses. I have mountains, but no trees. I have water, but no fish. What am I?"

**Answer:** A map

**Riddle:** "The more you take, the more you leave behind. What am I?"

**Answer:** Footsteps

**Riddle:** "What has keys but no locks, space but no room, and you can enter but not go in?"

**Answer:** A keyboard

### Scoring Suggestions
- **Correct answer:** 3 points
- **Good question asked:** 1 point (max 2 per riddle)

### Tips
- Read riddles slowly and clearly
- Allow discussion and collaboration
- The journey to the answer is half the fun!

## Spelling Bee

**Players:** 2-20  
**Duration:** 10-15 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Easy to Expert

### Overview
A traditional spelling bee format with increasingly difficult words.

### How to Play

1. Host announces the word
2. Player must spell it aloud
3. Player may ask for:
   - Definition
   - Use in a sentence
   - Language of origin
4. Host reveals correct spelling

### Difficulty Levels

**Easy:**
- accommodation
- necessary
- separate
- definitely

**Medium:**
- conscientious
- bureaucracy
- Wednesday
- rhythm

**Hard:**
- onomatopoeia
- antidisestablishmentarianism
- supercalifragilisticexpialidocious
- pneumonoultramicroscopicsilicovolcanoconiosis

### Scoring Suggestions
- **Easy correct:** 1 point
- **Medium correct:** 2 points
- **Hard correct:** 5 points

### Tips
- Say words clearly
- Use in context before asking for spelling
- Award bonus points for words spelled backwards!

## Logorithmic

**Players:** 4-20  
**Duration:** 20-30 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Medium

### Overview
The ultimate test of brand recognition and memory! Players must draw famous logos from memory, then compare with the real thing.

### How to Play

1. **Host** taps NEW to reveal a brand name
2. **All players** draw the logo from memory (30-60 seconds)
3. **Host** counts down
4. **Players** reveal their drawings
5. **Host** taps REVEAL to show the actual logo
6. **Everyone votes** on their favorite drawing
7. **Award points** based on votes

### Example Brands

| Brand | Category | Drawing Challenge |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| Nike | Sportswear | That swoosh is harder than it looks! |
| Apple | Technology | Is the bite on the left or right? |
| Starbucks | Coffee | Who remembers the mermaid details? |
| McDonald's | Fast Food | How many arches? |
| Adidas | Sportswear | Which direction do the stripes go? |

### Scoring Suggestions
- **Most accurate:** 3 points
- **Most creative:** 2 points
- **Funniest:** 1 point
- **Participation:** 1 point

### What You'll Need
- Paper and pens/pencils for each player
- A flat surface to draw on
- Timer (phone works fine)

### Tips
- Encourage creativity over accuracy
- Take photos of the best drawings
- Adjust time limit based on logo complexity

### Variations
- **Speed Round** - 15 seconds only
- **Non-dominant hand** - Draw with your other hand
- **Eyes closed** - Draw without looking at the paper

## Price is Right

**Players:** 2-20  
**Duration:** 15-20 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Medium

### Overview
Inspired by the classic TV game show! Guess the price of real Amazon products. Closest without going over wins!

### How to Play

1. **Host** taps NEW to show a product
2. **All players** write down their guess
3. **Host** asks for guesses one by one
4. **Host** taps REVEAL to show actual price
5. **Closest without going over** gets the points

### Example Products

| Product | Category | Actual Price |
|---------|----------|--------------|

### Scoring Rules (The Price is Right Rules)
- **Closest without going over:** 2 points
- **Exact price:** 5 points (bonus!)
- **Everyone over:** Closest to the price gets 1 point

### Tips for Host

## Chaos Mode

**Not a game mode, but a special feature available in all games!**

### Forfeits

Random punishments for losing players or rule breakers:

**Low:**
- Sing a song chosen by the group
- Tell your most embarrassing story
- Do your best impression

**Medium:**
- Take a shot
- Lose 3 points
- Swap seats with someone

**High:**
- Lose 5 points
- Wear something silly
- Let group choose your profile picture

**Extreme:**
- Take 5 shots (pace yourself!)
- Let group post on your social media
- Various wild challenges

### Challenges

Group activities and tasks:
- Group selfie with a silly pose
- Everyone does 10 jumping jacks
- Simultaneous dance party
- Group sing-along

### Rewards

Power-ups and bonuses:
- Steal 2 points from any player
- Immunity for one round
- Double points next round
- Choose the next category
